# Desirability - United Kingdom

## Overview

The UK represents a **highly desirable market** for Rafiq AI with a large teacher population, mature coaching culture, and well-structured school system. The Multi-Academy Trust (MAT) consolidation creates enterprise sales opportunities, while the established but underutilized coaching tradition presents a clear market gap for AI-powered solutions.

**Score: 4/5**

## 2. Coaching & CPD Culture

### Current State of CPD in UK (2025)

**The CPD Paradox:**
- 1 in 4 teachers received <1 day of formal CPD in 2024/25
- 40% say CPD didn't clearly improve ability to perform role
- **Coaching = "most impactful but least used" CPD format**
- Access to and impact of CPD remains uneven across England

**What Teachers Want:**
- Relevant and personalized CPD
- Based and embedded in school culture
- NOT one-off training events
- Job-embedded, ongoing development
- Practical application to classroom

### Instructional Coaching Evidence Base

**Strong Research Foundation:**
- UK has mature understanding of instructional coaching
- Increasingly recognized as effective job-embedded CPD
- Clear evidence base for improving teacher quality and pupil outcomes
- Quote: "In terms of impact on student outcomes, instructional coaching has a better evidence base than any other form of CPD" (Steve Farndon, 2019)

**What is Instructional Coaching (UK Definition):**
- Trained coach working individually with teacher
- Helps learn and adopt new teaching practices
- Evidence-based approach
- Focus on incremental improvement
- Safe space for development (not appraisal)

**Key Characteristics:**
- One-to-one or small group
- Regular, sustained over time
- Based on observation and feedback
- Tied to specific teaching practices
- Non-judgmental, developmental

### Coaching Culture Maturity

**Positive Factors:**
1. **Government Support:**
   - Early Career Framework (ECF) includes coaching
   - 2-year structured support for all new teachers
   - 5% off-timetable time in year 2
   - Coaching recognized in DfE policy

2. **Evidence-Based Approach:**
   - UK schools value research-backed interventions
   - Education Endowment Foundation (EEF) provides evidence standards
   - Chartered College of Teaching promotes evidence-informed practice
   - Schools ask "what's the evidence?" before purchasing

3. **Existing Models:**
   - Lesson Study (collaborative teacher inquiry)
   - Professional Learning Communities (PLCs)
   - Joint Practice Development (JPD)
   - Teaching School Hubs (87 hubs across England)

4. **Cultural Readiness:**
   - Teachers expect ongoing development
   - Professional development seen as right, not privilege
   - Collaborative culture in many schools
   - Openness to peer learning

**Challenges:**
1. **Implementation Gap:**
   - Known to be effective, but underutilized
   - Time constraints limit adoption
   - Coaching seen as resource-intensive
   - Schools struggle to scale 1-to-1 models

2. **Workload Concerns:**
   - Teachers working 50-59 hours/week
   - Adding coaching seen as "one more thing"
   - Solution: Position as workload REDUCER not adder

3. **Language Barrier:**
   - "Instruction" has directive connotation in UK (vs neutral in US)
   - Prefer "pedagogy," "teaching," "practice"
   - Need UK-appropriate terminology

4. **Low-Stakes Culture Essential:**
   - Coaching must be separate from appraisal/performance management
   - Need psychological safety
   - Developmental, not judgmental
   - Schools with high-stakes culture struggle with coaching

### Lesson Study Tradition

**What is Lesson Study:**
- Teacher-driven collaborative inquiry process
- Study curriculum, teaching, and student learning
- Originated in Japan, adopted in UK over 20+ years
- Small groups plan, observe, and refine lessons together

**UK Adoption:**
- Widespread practice in UK schools
- 25 different terminologies related to teacher collaboration in literature
- Most frequently cited: Professional Learning Communities (PLCs), Communities of Practice (CoPs), Lesson Study
- Evidence: Facilitator focus on student thinking, quality materials, and duration significantly impact effectiveness

**Relevance to Rafiq AI:**
- Lesson Study uses video observation
- Teachers already comfortable with recorded lessons
- Collaborative reflection culture exists
- AI could enhance/scale lesson study process

### Early Career Framework (ECF) - Major Policy Driver

**What is ECF:**
- Mandatory 2-year structured support for all early career teachers (ECTs)
- Launched nationally April 2022
- Part of DfE Teacher Recruitment and Retention Strategy (2019)
- Includes 5% funded off-timetable time in year 2

**2025 Updates:**
- NEW: Early Career Teacher Entitlement (ECTE) from September 2025
- Based on updated Initial Teacher Training and Early Career Framework (ITTECF)
- Combines ITT Core Content Framework and ECF
- Updates include: SEND, oracy, early cognitive development, evidence literacy

**Lead Providers:**
- UCL Institute of Education confirmed as lead provider
- Supported 18,000+ teachers since 2021
- Coaching is integral to ECF delivery

**Strategic Implication:**
- ECF = 50,000-60,000 new teachers entering annually
- All require coaching/mentoring in first 2 years
- Schools already budgeting for ECF delivery
- Rafiq AI can position as "ECF-aligned"
- Scalable coaching for ECT surge

## 8. Decision-Making Process

### Typical B2B School Sales Cycle

**Timing:**
- Budget setting: January-March (for September start)
- Procurement: April-June
- Implementation: July-September (summer prep)
- Academic year: September-August

**Stakeholders (Individual School):**
1. Headteacher / Principal (final decision)
2. Deputy Head for Teaching & Learning
3. CPD Lead / Director of Professional Development
4. Budget Holder / Business Manager
5. IT Manager (technical feasibility)
6. SENCO (if accessibility features needed)

**Stakeholders (MAT):**
1. CEO / Executive Principal (strategic decision)
2. Director of Education / Head of School Improvement
3. Director of Professional Development (key champion)
4. CFO (budget approval)
5. Data/IT Director (technical)
6. Individual Headteachers (implementation)

**Decision Criteria:**
1. Evidence of impact (most important)
2. Alignment with DfE standards/frameworks
3. Data protection and safeguarding compliance
4. Value for money (ROI calculation)
5. Ease of implementation
6. Ongoing support and training
7. References from similar schools/MATs

**Sales Cycle Length:**
- Individual School: 3-6 months
- MAT: 6-12 months (longer but larger contracts)
- Teaching School Hub: 4-8 months
