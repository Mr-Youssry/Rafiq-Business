# Turkey - Legal & Regulatory Environment

## Section Score: 4/5

Turkey offers a relatively business-friendly legal environment for foreign EdTech companies. The country allows 100% foreign ownership, has GDPR-aligned data protection laws, and is developing a modern AI regulatory framework. The main challenges are currency volatility and navigating the Turkish bureaucratic system.

---

## 1.1 General Legal Framework

### Business Entity Requirements

**Company Formation:**
- **LLC (Limited Şirket)**: Most common for startups
  - Minimum capital: TRY 50,000 (~$1,500 USD)
  - 1-50 shareholders allowed
  - 100% foreign ownership permitted
  - At least 1 manager (can be shareholder)
- **Joint Stock Company (Anonim Şirket)**: For larger ventures
  - Minimum capital: TRY 250,000 (~$7,500 USD)
  - Required for IPO eligibility

**Registration Process:**
- Online registration via MERSIS (Central Trade Registry System)
- Articles of Association in Turkish, notarized
- Foreign documents require apostille and certified Turkish translation
- Timeline: 5-10 business days
- Corporate bank account required for capital deposit

**Key Documents Required:**
- Articles of Association
- Shareholder ID/passports (apostilled for foreigners)
- Tax registration
- Chamber of Commerce registration
- Signature circular (notarized)

### Foreign Investment Rights

Turkey provides equal treatment for foreign and domestic investors:
- No restrictions on foreign ownership percentage in most sectors
- No requirement for Turkish citizen partners or directors
- Full profit repatriation allowed
- Protection under bilateral investment treaties

**Restricted Sectors (requiring ministry approval):**
- Defense and aviation
- Banking and insurance
- Broadcasting
- Education sector requires MEB notification but no special license for EdTech software

### Taxation

| Tax Type | Rate | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Corporate Income Tax | 25% | Standard rate (2024) |
| VAT | 20% | Standard; 10% for educational services |
| Withholding Tax | 10-15% | On dividends to foreign shareholders |
| Personal Income Tax | 0% to 40% | Progressive brackets |

**Incentives:**
- Technopark companies: Corporate tax exemption on software/R&D income
- R&D Tax Credits: 100% deduction on R&D expenses
- Investment Incentives: Various regional and sector-specific schemes

## 1.2 Data Protection & Privacy (KVKK)

### Overview

Turkey's data protection law is **KVKK (Kişisel Verilerin Korunması Kanunu)** - Law No. 6698, effective since 2016.

**Key Features:**
- Closely aligned with EU GDPR
- Applies to all entities processing Turkish citizens' data
- Enforced by the KVKK Authority (Personal Data Protection Authority)

### 2025 Updates

Recent amendments strengthen alignment with GDPR:
- **Enhanced breach notification**: 72-hour notification requirement
- **Expanded sensitive data categories**: Biometric, genetic, and location data
- **New data subject rights**: Data portability, right to object to automated decisions
- **Cross-border transfer guidance**: Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) framework

### Compliance Requirements for EdTech

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Lawful basis | Consent, contract, or legitimate interest |
| Data subject rights | Access, rectification, erasure, portability |
| Privacy notice | Clear disclosure in Turkish |
| Data processor agreements | Required for third-party processing |
| DPO | Not mandatory but recommended |
| Registration | Data controllers must register with KVKK Authority |
| Cross-border transfers | Requires adequacy decision or SCCs |

### Children's Data

- Parental consent required for data processing of minors (under 18)
- Enhanced protections for educational data
- Schools typically act as data controllers; EdTech platforms as processors

## 1.3 AI Regulations

### Current Status (2025)

Turkey is in a **transitional phase** for AI regulation:
- Draft AI Bill under parliamentary review
- Risk-based approach inspired by EU AI Act
- National AI Strategy in place

### Draft AI Law Key Provisions

| Aspect | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| Risk classification | High-risk systems require additional compliance |
| Registration | High-risk AI must register with supervisory authority |
| Risk management | Mandatory internal audits and documentation |
| Transparency | Users must be informed when interacting with AI |
| Accountability | Clear liability framework for AI decisions |
| Principles | Safety, openness, fairness, privacy protection |

### AI in Education

- No specific restrictions on AI use in education currently
- Ministry of National Education (MEB) has shown openness to digital innovation
- AI curriculum being rolled out in schools (initiative from MEB)
- Oracle partnership for AI training (50,000 trainees)

### Practical Implications for Rafiq AI

- Current environment: Favorable for AI EdTech deployment
- Recommendation: Build transparency and explainability into product
- Monitor: Parliamentary progress on AI Bill for future compliance

## 1.4 Education Sector Regulations

### Ministry of National Education (MEB)

The **Ministry of National Education (Milli Eğitim Bakanlığı - MEB)** governs all education in Turkey.

**EdTech Products:**
- No specific certification or accreditation required for educational software
- Digital platforms are treated as supplementary educational materials
- Private schools have autonomy in selecting digital tools
- Public schools typically use MEB-approved platforms (EBA, Vitamin)

### Selling to Schools

**Private Schools:**
- Independent purchasing decisions
- Direct B2B sales model
- Decision makers: School principals, IT directors, academic coordinators
- Sales cycle: 2-6 months typical

**Public Schools:**
- Centralized procurement through MEB
- Tender process for large contracts
- Local discretion for small purchases
- Budget allocation through provincial education directorates

### Digital Infrastructure

- **EBA (Eğitim Bilişim Ağı)**: MEB's official digital learning platform
- **MEBBİS**: Information system for teacher management and development
- **ÖBA**: Teacher professional development portal
- Integration with existing platforms may facilitate adoption

## 1.5 Employment & Work Permits

### Hiring in Turkey

**Work Permits for Founders:**
- Required for non-Turkish nationals planning to reside and work in Turkey
- Application through Ministry of Labor
- Tied to company sponsorship
- Processing: 30-60 days

**Remote Sales:**
- No local presence required for B2B SaaS sales
- Can operate remotely for initial market validation
- Local entity recommended once revenue reaches significant levels

**Saudization Equivalent:**
- No strict localization quotas for tech companies
- Technopark companies have flexibility in hiring foreign talent

## 1.6 Intellectual Property

### IP Protection Framework

| Type | Law | Duration |
|------|-----|----------|
| Patents | Industrial Property Law No. 6769 | 20 years |
| Trademarks | Industrial Property Law No. 6769 | 10 years (renewable) |
| Copyright | Law No. 5846 | Life + 70 years |
| Software | Protected as literary work | Life + 70 years |

**Key Points:**
- Turkey is signatory to major international IP treaties (WIPO, Paris Convention, Berne Convention)
- Turkish Patent and Trademark Office (TÜRKPATENT) handles registrations
- Software source code protected without registration
- Trademark registration recommended before market entry

## 1.7 Currency & Financial Considerations

### Currency Volatility

The Turkish Lira (TRY) has experienced significant depreciation:
- High inflation environment (60%+ in 2024)
- USD/TRY volatility requires careful pricing strategy

**Implications:**
- Consider USD or EUR-denominated contracts
- Local pricing may need quarterly adjustment
- Customer willingness to pay affected by economic conditions

### Payment Processing

- Major payment processors available (iyzico, PayTR, Stripe via partner)
- Credit card penetration high in urban areas
- Bank transfer common for B2B

## Key Takeaways

### Strengths
- 100% foreign ownership allowed
- GDPR-aligned data protection
- No specific EdTech licensing requirements
- Strong technopark incentives
- Modern AI regulatory approach in development

### Challenges
- Currency volatility and economic uncertainty
- Bureaucratic processes can be slow
- Turkish language requirement for legal documents
- Public school sales require MEB alignment

### Recommendations

1. **Start with private schools**: Simpler procurement, faster decisions
2. **Consider technopark registration**: Tax benefits, credibility
3. **Price in USD/EUR**: Protect against Lira volatility
4. **Build KVKK compliance**: Prepare for GDPR-equivalent requirements
5. **Monitor AI legislation**: Position for compliance as law develops
