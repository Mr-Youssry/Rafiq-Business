# Turkey - Feasibility (Can We Execute?)

## Section Score: 3/5

Turkey presents moderate execution feasibility. The startup ecosystem is active with strong government support programs, but the market requires Turkish language localization and ideally local partnerships. Remote sales are possible initially, but scaling requires local presence.

## 3.2 Sales & Operations

### Sales Model Assessment

| Model | Viability | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Remote sales (from abroad) | Medium | Initial validation possible |
| Partner/distributor model | High | Leverage existing relationships |
| Local sales rep | High | Required for scale |
| Direct enterprise sales | Low-Medium | Needs local trust-building |

### Remote Sales Viability

**Possible for Initial Phase:**
- SaaS products can be sold remotely
- Video demos effective for tech-forward schools
- English-speaking international schools accessible

**Limitations:**
- Private school decision makers prefer in-person meetings
- Turkish language demos essential for most schools
- Payment processing more complex across borders
- Public school sales nearly impossible remotely

### Recommended Sales Approach

**Phase 1: Remote Validation (Months 1-6)**
- Target international schools with English-speaking staff
- LinkedIn outreach to school principals and academic directors
- Virtual demos and pilot programs
- Goal: 5-10 pilot schools

**Phase 2: Local Partnership (Months 7-12)**
- Partner with local EdTech company or distributor
- Consider strategic partnership with Sebit or education consultancy
- Attend Education Technologies Summit (February)
- Goal: Establish local sales channel

**Phase 3: Local Presence (Year 2+)**
- Hire Turkey-based sales representative
- Consider Istanbul office or technopark presence
- Build MEB relationships for public school expansion

### Outreach Methods

| Channel | Effectiveness | Cost |

### Operational Requirements

**Localization:**
- Turkish language product essential (95%+ of teachers)
- Turkish curriculum alignment helpful
- Arabic numeral and Turkish character support
- RTL not required (Turkish uses Latin script)

## Key Takeaways

### Strengths

1. **Active startup ecosystem** ($1.4B+ investment in 2024)
2. **Government support programs** (TÜBİTAK BİGG)
3. **Strong angel investor network** (500+ in TBAA)
4. **Improving digital infrastructure**
5. **Partner opportunities** (established EdTech players)

### Challenges

1. **Turkish language essential** (localization investment)
2. **Local presence needed for scale** (sales, customer success)
3. **Not priority market for international VCs**
4. **Currency volatility** complicates planning
5. **Unknown personal network** (need to build connections)

### Recommendations

1. **Partner-first approach**: Find local distributor or strategic partner
2. **Start with international schools**: English-accessible pilot customers
3. **Apply to accelerators**: Startupbootcamp, KWORKS for network access
4. **Attend Education Technologies Summit**: Key annual event (February)
5. **Consider technopark setup**: If committing to Turkey long-term
6. **Budget for localization**: Turkish product version non-negotiable
