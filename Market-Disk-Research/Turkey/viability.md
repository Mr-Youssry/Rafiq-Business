# Turkey - Viability (Profit Potential)

## Section Score: 3/5

Turkey offers viable unit economics for an AI coaching product, but requires Turkey-specific pricing significantly lower than Western markets. The large teacher population creates scale opportunity, but lower willingness-to-pay and currency volatility require careful financial planning.

## 4.4 Break-Even Analysis

### Break-Even Calculation

**Fixed Costs (Turkey-focused operations):** ~$100,000/year

**Contribution Margin:**
- Average revenue per user: ~$50/year (blended)
- Variable cost per user: ~$8/year
- Contribution margin: $42/user (84%)

**Break-Even Point:**
- $100,000 ÷ $42 = **~2,400 paying users**
- OR ~35-40 schools (averaging 60 teachers each)

## Key Takeaways

### Strengths

1. **Scale opportunity** (1.2M teachers, 75K schools)
2. **Healthy unit economics** (LTV:CAC 3:1+)
3. **No direct competitor** (pricing flexibility)
4. **Multiple segments** (individual + B2B)
5. **Expansion potential** (MEB contracts later)

### Challenges

1. **Lower price points** than Western markets
2. **Currency volatility** complicates planning
3. **Longer break-even** than US/UK
4. **Localization required** for full market access
5. **Economic sensitivity** affects renewals

### Recommendations

1. **Start with international schools**: Higher prices, no localization needed
2. **Dual pricing model**: USD for international, TRY for local
3. **Freemium for individuals**: Build awareness before monetization
4. **Annual contracts**: Lock in revenue, reduce currency risk
5. **Partner for distribution**: Reduce CAC through existing channels
