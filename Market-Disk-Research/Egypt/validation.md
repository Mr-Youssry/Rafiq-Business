## 5.2 Customer Understanding

### Customer Segments Defined

| Segment | Description | Size | Priority |
|---------|-------------|------|----------|
| Individual Teachers | Self-motivated educators | ~200K active | Medium |
| Private School Teachers | Teachers at private schools | ~100K | High |
| School Leaders/Admins | Principals, PD coordinators | ~15K | High |
| International School Staff | Teachers at international schools | ~20K | High |
| Ministry/Government | Policy makers, district leaders | ~1K | Low (long-term) |

### Why This Problem Matters in Egypt

**Teacher Pain Points:**
1. **Overcrowded classrooms** - 43-130 students, impossible to get feedback
2. **Low salaries** - Push toward private tutoring, less investment in growth
3. **Heavy curriculum burden** - 32 subjects, no time for development
4. **Limited autonomy** - Centralized system, scripted teaching
5. **Isolation** - No peer coaching culture, limited collaboration
6. **Poor training quality** - Workshop-based, not practical

### Problem Frequency and Cost

| Impact | Frequency | Cost |
|--------|-----------|------|
| Burnout | Daily | High turnover, low morale |
| Poor teaching quality | Daily | Student outcomes suffer |
| Reliance on tutoring | Weekly | Teachers supplement income |
| Limited career growth | Ongoing | Brain drain to other sectors |

### Evidence of Problem
- 469,000 teacher shortage (2024)
- UNICEF: Majority of students lack basic reading skills
- Egypt ranked 42/43 in international reading study (2021)
- 120-day school year vs 190 international standard
- Teacher salary as low as EGP 1,000/month (part-time)

## 5.7 Willingness to Pay Validation

### WTP Drivers in Egypt

| Driver | Impact | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Perceived career benefit | High | Tie to promotions, certifications |
| Time savings | Medium | Busy teachers value efficiency |
| Quality improvement | Medium | Better outcomes = less stress |
| Social proof | High | Testimonials from peers |
| Affordability | Critical | Must be accessible |

### Price Sensitivity Testing Plan

**Method: Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter**

Questions to ask:
1. At what price is this too expensive to consider?
2. At what price does this seem like a bargain?
3. At what price does this start to seem expensive but still acceptable?
4. At what price does this seem too cheap to be quality?

**Initial Price Points to Test:**
- EGP 49/month (bargain)
- EGP 99/month (affordable)
- EGP 199/month (premium individual)
- EGP 299/month (school license/teacher)

### Value Proposition Refinements Based on WTP

| If WTP is... | Then adjust... |
|--------------|----------------|
| Lower than expected | Add more value, reduce scope |
| Higher than expected | Premium tier opportunity |
| Varies by segment | Tiered pricing |
| Near zero | Pivot to B2B only |

### Pricing Strategy Adjustments

Based on Egypt market research:
- **B2C:** Start at EGP 99/month, freemium for acquisition
- **B2B:** Start at EGP 200-300/teacher/month for private schools
- **International:** EGP 500-1,000/teacher/month premium
- **Discounts:** Annual plans (2 months free), volume discounts
