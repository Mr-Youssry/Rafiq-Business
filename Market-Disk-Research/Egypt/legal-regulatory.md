# Egypt - Legal & Regulatory Environment

## 1.1 General Legal Framework

### Business Regulations for Foreign Companies
- **100% foreign ownership** is permitted for LLCs and Joint Stock Companies
- Foreign companies can establish: LLC, Joint Stock Company (JSC), Branch Office, or Representative Office
- **General Authority for Investment and Free Zones (GAFI)** is the primary regulatory body for company registration
- All businesses must register with the Egyptian Commercial Registry

### Business Entity Requirements

| Entity Type | Min Capital | Min Shareholders | Notes |
|-------------|-------------|------------------|-------|
| LLC | EGP 30,000-50,000 | 2 | Resident manager required |
| JSC (Private) | EGP 250,000 | 3 | Shares can be traded |
| JSC (Public) | EGP 500,000 | 3 | Publicly traded |
| Branch Office | USD 100 | N/A | Extension of foreign company |
| One-Person Company | EGP 50,000 | 1 | Single owner |

### Registration Costs (Estimated)
- Government registration fees: EGP 500
- Notary fees: EGP 200
- Establishment fees: 0.1% of capital (min EGP 100, max EGP 1,000)
- Chamber of Commerce fees: 0.2% of capital (min EGP 24, max EGP 2,000)
- Legal services: EGP 5,000-10,000
- Annual renewal: EGP 3,500-6,000

### Tax Obligations
- Corporate income tax: **22.5%**
- VAT: **14%**
- Registration for Income Tax Card and VAT required

### 2025 Amendments
- Exemptions on development charges, registration fees, and stamp duty
- New requirements for Egyptian majority ownership in partnerships
- Mandatory registration for foreign companies operating in Egypt

## 1.2 Data Protection and Privacy Laws

### Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL)
- **Enacted:** 2020 (first comprehensive data protection law in Egypt)
- **Modeled after:** EU GDPR

### Key Requirements
- **Consent:** Explicit consent required before processing personal data
- **Data Subject Rights:** Access, correction, erasure, and withdrawal of consent
- **Breach Notification:** 72 hours to report to authorities and affected individuals
- **Processing Conditions:**
  - Data collected for legitimate, specific, declared purposes
  - Processing must be lawful and appropriate
  - Data retention limited to necessary period

### Sensitive Data Categories
- Health data (psychological, mental, physical, genetic)
- Biometric data
- Financial data
- Religious beliefs and political opinions
- Children's data (regardless of type)

### Penalties
- Fines up to **EGP 5 million**
- Imprisonment up to **3 years**

### Cross-Border Data Transfers
- Permitted where protection level meets Egyptian standards
- Requires license/permit from the Personal Data Protection Center

### Enforcement Status (2024)
- Limited enforcement due to delayed operation of PDPC
- No formal administrative penalties issued yet
- Privacy litigation in early stages

## 1.3 AI-Specific Regulations

### National AI Strategy (2025-2030)
- **Launched:** January 2025 by President El-Sisi
- **Target:** ICT sector to contribute 7.7% of GDP by 2030
- **Position:** Regional AI hub for Africa and Middle East

### Six Strategic Pillars
1. Governance
2. Infrastructure
3. Technology
4. Data
5. Ecosystem
6. Talent

### Current Regulatory Framework
| Regulation | Status | Impact on AI |
|------------|--------|--------------|
| PDPL (2020) | Active | Privacy, consent, data handling |
| Electronic Transactions Law (2020) | Active | Digital operations |
| Egyptian Charter for Responsible AI | Active | Ethics guidelines |
| IP Law (2024 Amendment) | Active | Algorithm/software protection |
| AI & Data Exchange Regulations | Drafting | Risk classification, oversight |
| Cloud First Policy (2024) | Active | Data localization for classified data |

- Aligned with UNESCO, ISO/IEC 42001 standards
- Egypt leads Arab AI Group and African AI Group (African Union)
- Advanced ~50 positions in global AI rankings (2020-2024)

## 1.4 Education Sector Regulations

### Ministry Overview
- **Ministry of Education and Technical Education (MoETE)** oversees K-12
- **Ministry of Higher Education and Scientific Research** oversees universities
- 50,000+ public schools serving 22+ million students

### EdTech Regulatory Environment
- **No specific EdTech legislation** exists
- Education highly regulated but regulations don't always allow experimentation
- No EdTech-specific data protection (GDPR compliance recommended for differentiation)

### Accreditation Requirements
- Schools require Ministry of Education license for educational quality
- Content quality accreditation available (e.g., Discovery Education partnership 2018)
- Curriculum development and accreditation: **1-3 years** typical timeframe

### Procurement Requirements (Tenders & Bids Law 89/1998)
- Public tender or public negotiations required for government contracts
- **Egyptian Content Requirement:** 40% minimum Egyptian component (Law 5/2015)
- **Bid bond:** Up to 2% (refunded to unsuccessful bidders)
- **Final deposit:** Up to 5% (within 10 days of acceptance)
- E-procurement mandatory for contracts above $50,000
- **Central Agency for Public Tenders (CAPT)** is regulatory body

### Key Regulations
| Law/Policy | Focus |
|------------|-------|
| Education Law 155/2007 | Technology experts job descriptions |
| 2014 Constitution Article 19 | Innovation and scientific thinking |
| 2014 Constitution Article 25 | Digital literacy commitment |
| Strategic Plan 2014-30 | ICT in planning, evaluation, decision-making |

### Selling to Schools - Practical Considerations
- Ministerial approval may be required for variance from standard terms
- Partnership opportunities exist (Microsoft, World Bank, USAID models)
- Education Egypt expo: 250+ suppliers connect with decision makers annually
