# Strategic Validation - Georgia (USA)

## Key Assumptions to Test

### Assumption 1: Georgia Teachers Need AI Coaching

**Hypothesis:**
Georgia teachers, facing burnout (2/3 report burnout), shortage pressures, and limited coaching access, will embrace AI-powered instructional coaching as a solution.

**Evidence Supporting:**
- 2/3 of Georgia educators report burnout (PAGE survey)
- Retention dropped below 90%
- Only 60 schools have state-funded coaches (2.7% of schools)
- 31% unlikely to remain in education for 5+ years

**Evidence Against:**
- Some teachers may resist AI (trust issues)
- Preference for human connection
- Technology fatigue post-pandemic

**Validation Method:**
- 15 teacher interviews in metro Atlanta
- Survey of 100+ Georgia teachers
- Pilot program engagement metrics

**Go/No-Go Threshold:**
- 70%+ of interviewed teachers express interest
- 60%+ pilot engagement rate

### Assumption 2: Georgia Districts Will Pay for AI Coaching

**Hypothesis:**
Despite below-average PD spending, Georgia districts will allocate budget for AI coaching solutions that demonstrate ROI.

**Evidence Supporting:**
- State literacy coaching initiative shows budget for coaching
- 15% reading improvement validates coaching ROI
- Metro Atlanta districts have higher budgets
- ESSER sunset creates need for cost-effective solutions

**Evidence Against:**
- Georgia ranks 33rd in per-pupil spending
- Average teacher salary below national
- Competing budget priorities
- Free state coaching in some schools

**Validation Method:**
- 5 district curriculum director interviews
- Review of actual PD budgets (public records)
- Pilot-to-paid conversion rate

**Go/No-Go Threshold:**
- 3+ districts express budget availability
- 2+ pilot districts convert to paid

### Assumption 3: RESAs Will Partner with Rafiq AI

**Hypothesis:**
Georgia's 16 RESAs will serve as effective distribution partners, providing credibility and access to districts.

**Evidence Supporting:**
- RESAs are trusted PD providers
- RESAs seek innovative solutions to offer
- Northwest RESA already offers coaching endorsement
- RESAs have established district relationships

**Evidence Against:**
- RESAs may have competing priorities
- Partnership development takes time
- Some RESAs may prefer incumbent solutions

**Validation Method:**
- Outreach to 3 metro Atlanta RESAs
- Explore partnership models
- Test co-marketing approach

**Go/No-Go Threshold:**
- 1+ RESA expresses partnership interest
- Clear pathway to joint pilots

### Assumption 4: Georgia Market Leads to Southeast Expansion

**Hypothesis:**
Success in Georgia will create momentum for expansion to other Southeast states (Florida, North Carolina, South Carolina, Alabama, Tennessee).

**Evidence Supporting:**
- Georgia is regional hub
- GaETC attracts multi-state attendees
- Similar coaching cultures across Southeast
- Regional word-of-mouth networks

**Evidence Against:**
- Each state has unique regulations
- May not transfer directly
- Regional competitors may emerge

**Validation Method:**
- Track out-of-state inquiries from Georgia pilots
- Attend regional conferences
- Monitor competitor activity in Southeast

**Go/No-Go Threshold:**
- 2+ out-of-state inquiries from Georgia exposure
- Clear interest from adjacent state districts

## Willingness to Pay Validation

### WTP Drivers in Georgia

**Primary Drivers:**
1. **Time Savings:** Immediate feedback vs. waiting for coach
2. **Access:** Coaching previously unavailable
3. **Privacy:** Non-evaluative, confidential
4. **Effectiveness:** Tangible improvement in practice

**Secondary Drivers:**
1. **Career Growth:** Documentation for advancement
2. **Certification:** Potential PLU/CEU credits
3. **Community:** Connection with other teachers

### WTP Validation Approach

**Phase 1: Qualitative (Weeks 1-4)**
- 15 teacher interviews with pricing discussion
- 5 district leader budget conversations
- Van Westendorp price sensitivity analysis

**Phase 2: Quantitative (Weeks 5-8)**
- Survey to 100+ Georgia teachers
- A/B test pricing on landing page
- Conjoint analysis for feature-price trade-offs

**Phase 3: Behavioral (Weeks 9-12)**
- Pilot with premium tier offer
- Track upgrade conversion
- Measure price elasticity

### Pricing Strategy Adjustments

**If WTP is Lower Than Expected:**
- Reduce premium individual price to $59/year
- Offer annual-only (no monthly) to reduce churn
- Bundle with PLU credits to add value

**If WTP is Higher Than Expected:**
- Maintain pricing, increase feature set
- Add premium tier for power users
- Introduce district "premium" package
