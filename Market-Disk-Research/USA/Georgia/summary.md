## Executive Summary

Georgia represents an **excellent state-level market** for AI-powered instructional coaching within the broader USA strategy. With 114,800 teachers, a teacher retention crisis (dropped below 90%), and a state-funded literacy coaching initiative showing 15% reading improvement, the opportunity for scalable AI coaching is significant.

**Key Strengths:**
- 8th largest teacher population in USA (114,800 teachers)
- Urgent need: 2/3 of educators report burnout (PAGE survey)
- State commitment: Literacy coaching initiative in 60 schools with proven results
- Metro Atlanta concentration: 60% of teachers in tech-forward region
- Strong ecosystem: CREATE-X, ATDC, Atlanta Ventures, GaETC conference
- Clear regulatory framework: SB 89 provides compliance roadmap
- RESA network: 16 RESAs as distribution partners

**Key Challenges:**
- Below-average PD spending (ranked 33rd in per-pupil spending)
- Lower teacher salaries than top-tier states ($63,945 vs. $66,397 national)
- Long sales cycles typical of K-12 (6-18 months)
- Competition from free state coaching (60 schools)
- Need to build network from scratch

## Key Insights

### Opportunities

1. **Teacher Retention Crisis**
   - Retention dropped below 90% (89.21% in 2023)
   - 2/3 of educators report burnout
   - 31% unlikely to remain in education for 5+ years
   - Special education: 20% annual attrition rate

2. **State Coaching Initiative Success**
   - 60 schools with full-time literacy coaches
   - 15% improvement in student reading after 1 year
   - Strongest gains in kindergarten
   - Validates demand for coaching solutions

3. **Metro Atlanta Concentration**
   - 60% of teachers in metro Atlanta area
   - Higher salaries: Fulton ($60K-$110K), Cobb ($50K-$90K)
   - Tech-forward culture (Georgia Tech, startup ecosystem)
   - Efficient market development opportunity

4. **RESA Distribution Network**
   - 16 RESAs cover all Georgia districts
   - Trusted PD providers
   - Partnership opportunity for scaling

5. **Competitive Gap: Limited AI Coaching**
   - State coaching only in 60 schools (2.7% of 2,200 schools)
   - No dominant AI coaching player in Georgia
   - AI can scale proven coaching model statewide

### Challenges

1. **Below-Average PD Spending**
   - Georgia ranks 33rd in per-pupil spending
   - Estimated $3,000-$5,000/teacher PD (vs. $8,300 national)
   - Price sensitivity higher than CA, NY
   - **Mitigation:** Position as cost-effective alternative to human coaches

2. **Long Sales Cycles**
   - 6-18 months typical for district sales
   - 181 individual districts to target
   - Budget cycles constrain purchasing windows
   - **Mitigation:** RESA partnerships, freemium for individual teachers

3. **Building Network from Scratch**
   - No existing Georgia contacts (to be validated)
   - Need to establish credibility
   - Competition from established relationships
   - **Mitigation:** GaETC conference, CREATE-X/ATDC accelerators

4. **Competition from State Initiative**
   - 60 schools have free state-funded coaches
   - May reduce urgency in those schools
   - **Mitigation:** Target the other 2,140 schools without coaching
