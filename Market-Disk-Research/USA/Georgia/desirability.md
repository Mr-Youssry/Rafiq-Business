# Market Desirability - Georgia (USA)

## Teacher Shortage & Burnout Crisis

### Current Shortage Status

Georgia is among the hardest-hit states for teacher shortages nationally, alongside Florida and Alabama.

**2024-2025 Statistics:**
- Retention rate dropped from 90.82% (2022) to 89.21% (2023)
- Retention now below 90% (significant threshold)
- 31% of educators unlikely to remain in education for 5+ years
- 2/3 of educators report feeling burned out (PAGE survey)
- 1/4 report major teacher shortage at their school

**Subject Area Shortages (2023-2024):**
- English (middle/high school)
- Math (middle/high school)
- Social Studies (middle/high school)
- Core subjects (elementary)
- Special Education (all grades) - 20% annual attrition rate

### Burnout Drivers (PAGE Survey of 3,400 Educators)

1. **Long Work Hours**
2. **Low Pay** (Georgia ranks 33rd in per-pupil spending)
3. **Student Misbehavior and Violence**
4. **Lack of Administrative Support**

### Special Education Crisis

- 20% annual attrition rate for special education teachers
- ~50% transition to general education after 2 years
- Critical shortage area across all grade levels
- High burnout due to additional demands

## Instructional Coaching Culture & Adoption

### State-Level Coaching Initiatives

**Georgia Literacy Coaching Initiative (2024-2025):**
- GaDOE placed full-time literacy coaches in 60 elementary schools
- Focused on lowest-performing 5% of schools
- Impacts 18,000+ students
- **Results after 1 year:** 15% improvement in student reading
- Strongest gains in kindergarten

**Coaching Model Structure:**
- Locally hired literacy and instructional coaches
- Unified statewide framework
- Nationally recognized training in structured literacy
- Focus on Science of Reading implementation

### Teacher Support and Coaching Endorsement

Georgia offers official Teacher Support and Coaching (TSC) Endorsement:
- Based on GaPSC Rule 505-3-.105
- Prepares educators to supervise clinical practice
- Enables mentoring/coaching of proteges
- Dual endorsement: TSC + Coaching Endorsement

### Coaching Adoption Indicators

**Positive Signs:**
- State-funded coaching initiative demonstrates commitment
- GaDOE partnership with ANet (Achievement Network)
- RESAs offer coaching support services (e.g., Northwest RESA)
- PAGE and GAE provide coaching-related professional development
- 15% reading improvement validates coaching effectiveness

**Coaching Maturity:** MODERATE-HIGH
- Not as mature as California or New York
- Strong state-level support and infrastructure
- Growing adoption post-pandemic
- Evidence-based results driving expansion

## Professional Development Investment

### State Budget Context

**Georgia Education Budget (FY 2024):**
- Education is largest portion of state budget
- Per-pupil spending: Ranked 33rd nationally
- Below national average investment

**Professional Development Allocation:**
- Specific per-teacher PD spending data not publicly available
- Estimated $3,000-$5,000/teacher/year (below national $8,300 average)
- Federal Title II-A funds supplement state/local spending

### Professional Development Organizations

**PAGE (Professional Association of Georgia Educators):**
- Georgia's largest educator association
- Entry-level teacher dues: ~$200/year
- Offers PD at little/no cost to participants
- Focus on student engagement and school culture
- Regional events, workshops, leadership training

**GAE (Georgia Association of Educators):**
- Teacher dues: ~$325/year (based on $35k salary)
- Provides legal assistance, PD, advocacy
- Instructional Professional Development Cadre
- State and local PD delivery

**Regional Education Service Agencies (RESAs):**
- 16 RESAs across Georgia
- Primary PD delivery mechanism for many districts
- Technology training and support
- Instructional coaching endorsement programs

### Federal Funding Sources

- Title II-A: Professional development funds
- Title I (1003): School improvement grants
- ESSER funds (sunsetting): Significant PD investment opportunity ending
