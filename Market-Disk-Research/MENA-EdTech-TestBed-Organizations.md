## 1. Royal/Government-Affiliated Education Organizations

### 1.1 Queen Rania Foundation for Education and Development (QRF) — Jordan

- **Website:** https://qrf.org/en
- **What they do:** Founded in 2013 by Her Majesty Queen Rania Al Abdullah. Focuses on transforming education in Jordan and the region through early childhood literacy, teacher development, and evidence-based decision-making in education policy. Works with parents on emergent literacy skills, engages teachers on Arabic literacy, and advocates for evidence use in education.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** QRF has deep infrastructure for piloting and evaluating edtech, a research-first culture, and direct influence on Jordanian education policy. They conduct national-level surveys on technology use in schools and actively seek edtech solutions with evidence of impact.
- **Known edtech programs and pilots:**
  - Partnered with Amira Learning on an AI-supported reading program rolled out across Jordanian primary schools. The technology listens to students read aloud, provides immediate feedback, and generates progress data for teachers.
  - Published the "EdTech in Jordan's Schools" report based on National Teacher Survey findings (August 2025), mapping technology use across the country.
  - Runs evidence-gathering programs with Open Development & Education (OpenDevEd).
- **Related entity — Queen Rania Teacher Academy (QRTA):** https://qrta.edu.jo/en — Focuses on teacher professional development and training; a potential channel for edtech products aimed at teacher productivity.
- **Partnership pathway:** Apply through QRF's research and innovation programs. Demonstrate evidence of learning impact. QRF values RCTs and quasi-experimental designs.

### 1.2 Crown Prince Foundation (CPF) — Jordan

- **Website:** https://cpf.jo/en/homepage/
- **What they do:** Empowers youth with knowledge, skills, and experience through three pathways: Economic Participation, Leadership, and Sustainable Community Service. Runs innovation grants and entrepreneurship programs.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Provides innovation grants, operates makerspace programs, and runs coding/digital skills initiatives. Direct pathway to Jordanian youth demographic.
- **Known edtech programs:**
  - **JoCodes:** Free online program to learn coding and digital skills in Arabic and English, with globally recognized certificates.
  - **Makerspace Program** with the Maker Map — a national interactive platform connecting makers and innovators.
  - **Jordan Young Scientist (JoYS):** National science and innovation exhibition focusing on AI, robotics, biotechnology, renewable energy.
- **Partnership pathway:** Innovation grants and partnership applications through CPF. Focus areas include digital skills and youth empowerment.

### 1.3 Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum Knowledge Foundation (MBRF) — UAE

- **Website:** https://mbrf.ae/en
- **What they do:** Founded by Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum, Vice President of the UAE. Focuses on knowledge, education, entrepreneurship, innovation, and employment across the Arab world.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** One of the most prominent Arab knowledge institutions. Runs the largest Arabic e-learning platform, partners with UNDP, and hosts the annual Knowledge Summit. Significant reach across all Arab countries.
- **Known edtech programs:**
  - **Madrasa e-Learning Platform:** Offers 5,000+ free Arabised educational videos in science, math, biology, chemistry, and physics for KG to grade 12. Accessible to over 50 million Arab students. Expanding into Arabic language, computer science, AI, and space science.
  - **FutureSkills4All Initiative:** Joint venture with UNDP and Coursera focused on regional skills development for the next generation of Arab workforce.
  - **Knowledge Award:** Recognizes innovations in knowledge dissemination globally.
  - **Oxford Graduate Scholarship:** In collaboration with University of Oxford.
- **Partnership pathway:** Partner with MBRF's Madrasa platform for content integration, or participate in the Knowledge Summit for visibility and networking.

### 1.4 Abdulla Al Ghurair Foundation for Education (AGF) — UAE

- **Website:** https://www.alghurairfoundation.org/
- **What they do:** One of the largest privately funded philanthropic foundations in the Arab region. Abdulla Al Ghurair pledged one-third of his wealth (over $1 billion) for education. Supports high-quality technology-based education and skills development for youth transitioning to higher education and the labor market.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Massive scale (target: 15,000 youth over 10 years via STEM scholarships), direct partnerships with UAE Ministry of Education, and focus on technology-based education. Runs multiple edtech programs with measurable outcomes.
- **Known edtech programs:**
  - **TechUp Program:** Partnership with Udacity providing employment learning pathways — foundational digital literacy plus specialized technical degrees. Part of the NOMU initiative to upskill 25,000 Emirati youth by 2025.
  - **Takween Al Ghurair: STEM Connect:** With Discovery Education — provides students aged 13-18 with digital skill-based learning in and after school.
  - **MIT Bootcamp:** Partnership with UAEU Science and Innovation Park for 10-week innovation bootcamps.
  - **UCQOL:** Joint initiative with UAE Ministry of Education.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through AGF's existing partnerships framework. They actively seek edtech providers for their NOMU and STEM Connect programs.

### 1.5 Education Above All Foundation (EAA) — Qatar

- **Website:** https://www.educationaboveall.org/
- **What they do:** Founded in 2012 by Sheikha Moza bint Nasser (former First Lady of Qatar). Works across 77 countries to ensure equitable access to quality education, especially in poor and distressed communities. Focuses on out-of-school children and marginalized populations.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Massive geographic reach, focus on humanitarian/underserved contexts, and active collaboration with WISE and Qatar Foundation on AI edtech testbeds. Particular interest in affordable, locally appropriate, user-centered edtech.
- **Known edtech programs:**
  - **Digital School:** Creates alternate, accredited hybrid learning solutions for out-of-school adolescents (piloted in Zambia and other countries).
  - **Qatar AI Edtech Testbeds:** Collaboration with Qatar Foundation and WISE to establish testbeds within Qatari schools for AI-driven teaching and learning.
  - Develops humanitarian technology for education data collection, sharing, and impact measurement.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through EAA's partner network. They seek technology solutions for underserved populations, emphasizing affordability and evidence of impact.

## 2. Education Innovation Hubs and Accelerators

### 2.1 WISE Edtech Accelerator Programme — Qatar

- **Website:** https://www.wise-qatar.org/wise-works/edtech-accelerator/
- **What they do:** A flagship program of the World Innovation Summit for Education (WISE), an initiative of Qatar Foundation. Now in its sixth year, the accelerator has supported 46 ventures from over 20 countries, reaching 15+ million beneficiaries across 130 countries.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** The premier edtech accelerator in the MENA region. Provides direct access to Qatar's education ecosystem, the Ministry of Education, and WISE's global network. In 2025, introduced two tracks: Global (worldwide) and Local (Qatar-specific).
- **Known programs:**
  - **Global Track:** Supports impactful edtech ventures worldwide with mentorship, funding, and global visibility.
  - **Local Track:** Dedicated to startups ready to scale in Qatar — provides expert mentorship aligned with Qatari market needs, exclusive networking with decision-makers, and fast-tracked local funding.
  - **2025 Cohort:** 8 startups selected from 300+ applicants.
- **Partnership pathway:** Apply during open application windows (2025/2026 cycle announced). Applications are competitive; strong evidence of learning impact is valued.
- **Reference:** https://thepeninsulaqatar.com/article/11/03/2025/wise-edtech-accelerator-programme-focuses-on-local-education-startups

### 2.2 WISE Edtech Testbed — Qatar

- **Website:** https://www.wise-qatar.org/edtech-testbed/
- **What they do:** Empowers educators in Qatar by facilitating integration of AI tools in classrooms. Teachers experiment with AI-driven pedagogies, assess impact, and contribute to evidence-based decision-making. Operated in partnership with Qatar's Ministry of Education and Higher Education (MOEHE).
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** This is literally a testbed program. It uses a design-thinking approach where the Ministry identifies AI tools, and matchmaking is facilitated between tools, schools, and the Ministry. Two-cycle implementation per academic year.
- **Known programs:**
  - **Cycle 1:** First semester — initial integration and early-stage evaluation.
  - **Cycle 2:** Second semester — deeper implementation with preliminary findings shared globally.
  - Published report: "Piloting Qatar's First Edtech Testbed" documenting best practices and sustainable partnerships.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through the WISE Edtech Testbed interest form. The WISE-APPLICATION.md in this repository contains the application template. Demonstrate AI/edtech capabilities, target grades, and evidence of impact.

### 2.3 Dubai Future Accelerators (DFA) — UAE

- **Website:** https://www.dubaifuture.ae/initiatives/future-design-and-acceleration/dubai-future-accelerators
- **What they do:** Part of Dubai Future Foundation. Collaborates between startups, private entities, and the Dubai government. Positions Dubai as a destination for startups and a testbed for innovative technology across all sectors, including education.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Direct access to Dubai government entities as test bed partners. Startups work directly with government agencies to pilot solutions. Significant funding and infrastructure support.
- **Known programs:**
  - **TKS Innovation Program (2025-2026):** 10-month program for students aged 13-17 focusing on AI, robotics, and biotechnology.
  - **Dubai Future Academy:** 8 new training programs in 2025 covering foresight, AI, and innovation leadership.
  - **Dubai Future Experts Program (DEEP):** Government initiative for strategic foresight experts.
  - **RDI Grant Initiative:** Funds groundbreaking research and development.
- **Partnership pathway:** Apply through Dubai Future Accelerators program cycles. Education/edtech is an eligible sector.

### 2.4 Sharjah Startup Studio (S3) — UAE

- **What they do:** Four-month accelerator program (October 2025 – February 2026) supporting startups in EdTech, Sustainability, Advanced Manufacturing, and Creative Industries. Culminates in showcase at Sharjah Entrepreneurship Festival.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** EdTech is explicitly one of their four target sectors. Provides personalized mentorship, investor introductions, co-working spaces, software perks, and community networking.
- **Partnership pathway:** Apply during program cycles. EdTech startups are a priority sector.

### 2.5 Flat6Labs — Regional (Egypt, Tunisia, Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, Jordan, UAE)

- **Website:** https://www.flat6labs.com/
- **What they do:** MENA's leading seed and early-stage VC firm, founded in 2011 in Cairo. Has offices in Abu Dhabi, Beirut, Jeddah, Manama, and Tunis. Nearly 600 investments, 200 exits. EdTech and HealthTech are among top verticals for job creation.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Region-wide presence across six countries gives access to diverse MENA markets. Actively supports education startups. EdTech companies in their portfolio have created 1,200+ jobs across MENA.
- **Known programs:**
  - **StartMashreq Growth Track:** Selected 24 startups from Jordan, Lebanon, and Iraq, including edtech ventures.
  - **Flat6Labs Riyadh Accelerator:** First cohort graduated in 2023.
  - **Flat6Labs Tunis:** Active in Tunisia's growing edtech ecosystem.
- **Partnership pathway:** Apply to Flat6Labs in the country of interest. EdTech is a welcomed vertical.

### 2.6 Wamda Capital / Wamda X — Regional (UAE-based, MENA-wide)

- **Website:** https://www.wamda.com/
- **What they do:** Venture capital and accelerator supporting tech startups in MENA. Wamda X provides funding, resources, and mentorship through three phases: X START, X SOLVE, and X SCALE.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Broad MENA network, deep ecosystem connections, and track record of supporting education-adjacent startups.
- **Partnership pathway:** Apply through Wamda X programs or engage with Wamda Capital for investment.

### 2.7 iPARK — Jordan

- **Website:** https://www.ipark.jo/
- **What they do:** Jordan's leading innovation catalyst for startups. Supports entrepreneurs from ideation through growth stages and regional expansion. EdTech is a priority sector alongside ICT, healthtech, and fintech.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Jordan is a regional hub for Arabic-language digital innovation and edtech. iPARK leverages Jordan's strong technical talent pool.
- **Partnership pathway:** Apply to incubation/acceleration programs. Access to seed funding ($10,000-70,000), mentors, and regional investors.

### 2.8 MindCET — Israel

- **Website:** https://www.mindcet.org/en/
- **What they do:** World-leading EdTech innovation center founded in 2012. Brings together entrepreneurs, educators, and researchers. Independent body within the Center for Educational Technology (CET), Israel's market leader in technology-based education for 40+ years.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Operates the first edtech-dedicated accelerator in the world. Runs school pilots, has R&D lab, and global startup awards. Three divisions: The Garage (programs for developers), The Aquarium (research), and The Laboratory (school implementation).
- **Known programs:**
  - **Accelerator:** 10 companies selected annually from hundreds of applications. Focus on product-market fit.
  - **School Pilots:** Implements innovative pedagogy through significant pilots at schools.
  - **MindCETex:** R&D-based entrepreneurial program for developers.
  - **Global EdTech Startups Awards:** International competition.
- **Partnership pathway:** Apply to the accelerator (annual cycle) or seek school pilot partnerships.
- **Note:** While Israel is not typically grouped with MENA for business purposes, MindCET's programs are globally oriented and accept MENA-focused ventures.

## 3. Government Education Reform Bodies

### 3.1 Saudi Arabia — Ministry of Education + SDAIA (Saudi Data and AI Authority)

- **MOE Website:** https://www.moe.gov.sa/en
- **SDAIA Website:** https://sdaia.gov.sa/en
- **What they do:** The Ministry of Education, in collaboration with SDAIA and the Ministry of Communications, is rolling out a nationwide AI curriculum reaching 6+ million students starting in the 2025-2026 academic year. This is the largest AI-in-education initiative in MENA.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Massive scale (6 million+ students), government mandate for AI integration, and active piloting of edtech solutions. In 2024, a pilot AI elective engaged 50,000+ high schoolers providing feedback through real projects in healthcare and transportation.
- **Known programs:**
  - **National AI Curriculum:** Mandatory for all public schools, phased rollout from primary through high school.
  - **SAMAI Initiative:** Training teachers and citizens in AI tools; 500,000+ signed up, goal of 1 million.
  - **SDAIA Academy:** Training programs in data and AI.
  - **National Cross-Disciplinary Curriculum for Universities:** MOUs with 14 public and private universities for mandatory data/AI courses.
  - **Specialized AI Schools:** Developed with SDAIA and Tuwaiq Academy, expanding to five more regions.
  - **500,000+ teachers** undergoing mandatory professional training including 5,200+ training opportunities with international programs.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through the Ministry of Education's edtech procurement processes or SDAIA's partnership framework. International edtech companies with AI capabilities are particularly sought.

### 3.2 Saudi Arabia — Education and Training Evaluation Commission (ETEC)

- **Website:** https://etec.gov.sa/en
- **What they do:** Responsible for evaluation, assessment, and accreditation in education and training across both public and private sectors. Developed in collaboration with OECD to benchmark against international standards.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** ETEC's role as the evaluator means edtech products validated through ETEC channels gain credibility across the Saudi education system. They have built digital evaluation infrastructure that edtech companies can integrate with.
- **Known edtech initiatives:**
  - **Tamayuz Digital Platform:** Automates evaluation processes, streamlines data analysis, generates school evaluation reports.
  - **Mustaqbalhum Application:** Provides parents with access to children's test results and school evaluation information.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through ETEC's institutional quality assurance framework. Products that support evaluation, assessment, or school improvement are particularly relevant.

### 3.3 UAE — Abu Dhabi Department of Education and Knowledge (ADEK)

- **Website:** https://www.adek.gov.ae/
- **What they do:** Regulates and oversees all education in the Emirate of Abu Dhabi — public, private, and charter schools. Runs scholarship programs, summer programs, and curriculum initiatives.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Direct authority over Abu Dhabi's education system. Actively integrating digital tools and AI into education. Focus areas include AI, biotechnology, neurobiology, and sustainability.
- **Known programs:**
  - **ADEK Summer Program (2025):** 200 high-performing Emirati students in programs aligned with AI, biotechnology, neurobiology, and design.
  - **Abu Dhabi Scholarships (ADS):** Majors include AI, Machine Learning, Renewable Energy, FinTech, and Healthcare Biotech.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through ADEK's vendor/partner framework. Products aligned with UAE national identity, AI integration, and digital transformation priorities are prioritized.

### 3.4 Morocco — Ministry of National Education + Digital Lab

- **What they do:** The Digital Lab was established in November 2023 through a partnership between four ministries and the Deposit and Management Fund. Acts as a collaborative platform for developing and deploying digital solutions for pedagogical challenges.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Morocco's education system is undergoing massive digital transformation under the Digital Morocco 2030 strategy ($1.1 billion investment) and the Education Framework Law. The Digital Lab specifically seeks edtech solutions for remediation, language skills, lesson digitalization, and assessment.
- **Known programs:**
  - **Digital Morocco 2030 Strategy:** Targeting 10% GDP rise, creating 240,000 jobs, training 100,000+ youth annually in digital skills.
  - **Connected Schools Program:** Modernizing education through school connectivity.
  - **22,500 Digital Experts Training:** Through 12 universities with 144 new digital programs by 2027.
  - Morocco has actively invited foreign edtech companies to submit tenders for digitizing education (2024).
- **Partnership pathway:** Respond to Morocco's edtech procurement tenders. Engage with the Digital Lab or through international development organizations (e.g., GIZ's Digital Transformation Center Morocco).
- **Reference:** https://launchbaseafrica.com/2024/07/23/digitizing-education-morocco-invites-foreign-edtech-companies-to-submit-tenders/

### 3.5 Kuwait — Ministry of Education

- **What they do:** Implemented the "Digital Transformation Strategy for Education" (2023) mandating digital learning tools in all public schools. Running the 2025-2027 Education Reform Plan.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Education technology investments expected to grow 70% by 2026 vs. 2022. Government allocated ~$200 million for digital education. Over 60% of milestones in the reform plan already achieved.
- **Known programs:**
  - **2025-2027 Education Reform Plan:** Curriculum updates, interactive screens in schools, teacher upskilling with international partners.
  - **Digital Curriculum Development:** Replacing textbooks with tablets and interactive content for primary and secondary education.
  - Partnered with major tech firms (Microsoft Education, Google for Education, Blackboard, Canvas) for smartboards, learning apps, and digital exams.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through Ministry of Education procurement. Significant opportunity as 40% of teachers still express concerns about digital transition, creating demand for teacher-facing edtech tools.

### 3.6 Oman — Ministry of Education + Ministry of Higher Education, Research & Innovation (MoHERI)

- **MOE Website:** https://main.moe.gov.om/en
- **What they do:** Under Oman Vision 2040, constructed/refurbished 188 schools (2021-2025), implemented comprehensive plan to digitize curricula for grades 1-12, and launched the 'Noor' e-learning platform.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Active digitization of the entire K-12 curriculum creates demand for edtech content and tools. Significant government backing through Vision 2040.
- **Known programs:**
  - **Noor e-Learning Platform:** Interactive digital educational content accessible to all students.
  - **National Program for AI and Advanced Technologies** including the "Artificial Intelligence Makers" initiative.
  - **Industrial Innovation Academy:** Six strategic programs, training 360 Omanis as "Industrial Innovation Specialists."
  - **Ericsson Partnership:** Train-the-Trainer program at Center of Excellence for Advanced Telecommunications and IoT.
  - **OTS25 Conference:** Education and Smart Technologies Conference run by OMREN.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through MoHERI's partnership framework or through OMREN for education technology conferences and networking.

### 3.7 Egypt — Ministry of Education + USAID STEM Schools

- **What they do:** Running a comprehensive education reform aligned with Egypt Vision 2030, replacing rote memorization with competency-based learning. 14 STEM schools operating across Egypt (growing to 27). Allocated 30+ billion Egyptian pounds for education in 2025/2026.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Large scale (Egypt has the largest population in MENA). Active STEM school network serves as innovation incubators. Government is transforming 1,270 technical schools into applied technology institutions.
- **Known programs:**
  - **Egyptian Knowledge Bank (EKB):** National digital learning resource.
  - **STEM Schools Project (USAID-funded):** 14 schools teaching project-based curriculum focused on solving Egypt's grand challenges.
  - **STESSA (STEM Teacher Education and School Strengthening Activity):** $24.2 million USAID project through 21PSTEM, creating teacher and principal preparation programs at five Egyptian universities.
  - **Amideast STEM Center:** Established in 2021 with Boeing support for accessible, affordable STEM education.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through USAID-funded programs for STEM schools, or through the Ministry's applied technology school transformation initiative. International partnerships actively sought.

### 3.8 Bahrain — Tamkeen (Labour Fund)

- **Website:** https://www.tamkeen.bh/en
- **What they do:** Bahrain's Labour Fund supporting workforce development, entrepreneurship, and digital skills training. Strategic partner for edtech implementation across the country.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Actively funds and supports edtech initiatives. Training 50,000 Bahrainis in AI by 2030. Runs apprenticeship programs, advanced skills training, and entrepreneurship incubators.
- **Known programs:**
  - **Reboot01:** Bahrain-based coding institute (400 students), supported by Tamkeen, specializing in AI, cybersecurity, and FinTech.
  - **AI-powered Virtual Sim Patient:** Partnership between RCSI Bahrain and K-Labs Technology Solutions.
  - **AWS Cloud Innovation Centers:** At UOB and Bahrain Polytechnic, training 100+ students.
  - **Advanced Skills Program:** Fully funded tech training delivered by Localized in partnership with Skills Union.
  - **Mashroo3i 2.0:** Entrepreneurship program for young Bahrainis.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through Tamkeen's partner programs. They actively seek technology training providers and edtech solutions.

## 4. Major Education-Focused NGOs and Foundations

### 4.1 Qatar Foundation (QF) — Qatar

- **Website:** https://www.qf.org.qa/education
- **What they do:** Founded by Sheikh Hamad bin Khalifa Al Thani and Sheikha Moza bint Nasser. One of the most influential education organizations in the Middle East. Hosts Education City (home to branch campuses of Georgetown, Northwestern, Carnegie Mellon, and others). Parent organization of WISE.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Unparalleled education infrastructure, global network, and willingness to pilot innovative approaches. Hosts the HundrED Spotlight on Qatar program identifying and celebrating education innovations.
- **Known edtech programs:**
  - **WISE Edtech Accelerator** (covered above).
  - **WISE Edtech Testbed** (covered above).
  - **HundrED Spotlight on Qatar 2025:** Second cohort of 10 education innovators selected from 101 submissions. Focus areas include STEM, Arabic language, digital literacy, and sustainability.
  - **Qatar AI Edtech Testbeds:** Partnership with Education Above All and WISE to test AI tools in Qatari schools.
  - **Education House Live from Doha:** Events on AI for Human Flourishing and Global Collection 2026.
- **Partnership pathway:** Multiple entry points — WISE Accelerator, WISE Testbed, HundrED Spotlight applications, or direct engagement with QF's education division.

### 4.2 ADQ (Abu Dhabi sovereign wealth entity) + Gates Foundation Partnership — UAE/Global

- **Website:** https://www.adq.ae/
- **What they do:** ADQ is one of Abu Dhabi's largest holding companies. In December 2025, ADQ and the Gates Foundation announced a landmark $40 million, four-year partnership to accelerate responsible AI and edtech adoption for improving learning outcomes.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** This is the single largest dedicated AI-for-education fund announced in the region. While initially focused on sub-Saharan Africa, the partnership's AI-for-Education initiative is global, and ADQ's UAE base means regional edtech companies are well-positioned.
- **Known programs:**
  - **AI-for-Education:** Global initiative (launched 2022) developing practical models of AI-enabled learning and providing expert guidance to Global South governments.
  - **EdTech and AI Fund:** Multi-investor vehicle launching 2026 to scale proven edtech solutions nationally. First fund dedicated to national-level edtech expansion.
- **Key context:** 93% of edtech products in low and middle-income countries are not tested for proof-of-learning impact — creating massive demand for evidence-backed solutions.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage with ADQ's investment team or the EdTech and AI Fund when it launches in 2026. Solutions with proven learning impact data will be prioritized.
- **Reference:** https://www.gatesfoundation.org/ideas/media-center/press-releases/2025/12/education-systems-partnership

### 4.3 Education for Employment (EFE) Network — Regional

- **Website:** https://www.efe.org/
- **What they do:** Network of locally-run affiliates in Egypt, Jordan, Morocco, Palestine, Tunisia, and Yemen. Focuses on creating job opportunities for unemployed youth through professional and technical training.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Established presence in six MENA countries with direct access to youth populations. Strong focus on skills-based training that can integrate edtech solutions.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage with EFE country affiliates for training technology partnerships.

### 4.4 INJAZ Al-Arab — Regional (Jordan-based, MENA-wide)

- **What they do:** Part of Junior Achievement Worldwide. Operates across MENA to develop youth entrepreneurship. Runs the MyStartup Incubator providing office space, mentoring, and business development support.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Wide reach across MENA youth populations, entrepreneurship focus that welcomes edtech innovation, and established institutional relationships across the region.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through INJAZ's country programs or the MyStartup Incubator.

### 4.5 Lebanese Alternative Learning (LAL) — Lebanon

- **Website:** https://lal.ngo/
- **What they do:** EdTech NGO providing free quality education through the Tabshoura digital learning platform. Uses technology as an equalizing force for underprivileged communities. Innovates in offline solutions.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Operates in a challenging environment (Lebanon) where offline-capable and low-bandwidth edtech solutions are essential. Tests the resilience and accessibility of edtech products.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage directly through LAL's partnership framework. Particularly relevant for edtech with offline capabilities.

## 5. University-Based Innovation Centers

### 5.1 KAUST (King Abdullah University of Science and Technology) — Saudi Arabia

- **Website:** https://www.kaust.edu.sa/en/
- **What they do:** Saudi Arabia's premier research university. Operates the National Transformation Institute (NTI), the KAUST Entrepreneurship Center, and KAUST Academy. Training 1,500+ students from multiple Saudi universities annually.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** NTI bridges cutting-edge research with practical applications. Entrepreneurship Center provides deep tech startup programs. Strong connection to Saudi Vision 2030 goals.
- **Known programs:**
  - **National Transformation Institute (NTI):** Catalyst for innovation and economic growth; facilitates government-industry-academia collaborations.
  - **KAUST Academy:** Training in AI, bioinformatics, and renewable energy.
  - **Entrepreneurship Center:** World-class facilities, deep tech startup landing programs, acceleration, and connections to global innovation hubs.
  - **Entrepreneurship Adventures:** Free-access course for innovators.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through NTI for industry partnerships or through the Entrepreneurship Center for startup programs.

### 5.2 Mohammed VI Polytechnic University (UM6P) — Morocco

- **What they do:** First university in Africa to launch an AI-powered learning system (ChatGPT Edu). Leading institution for innovation and research in Morocco.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Pioneer in AI adoption for education in Africa/MENA. Strong research capabilities and institutional willingness to experiment with new technologies.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through UM6P's innovation and research programs.

### 5.3 Khalifa University (formerly Masdar Institute) — UAE

- **Website:** https://www.ku.ac.ae/
- **What they do:** Formed from the 2017 merger of Masdar Institute (developed with MIT), Petroleum Institute, and Khalifa University. Focus on advanced energy, sustainability, and technology.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** MIT heritage in innovation, strong research infrastructure, and focus on technology transfer.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through Khalifa University's research and innovation office.

### 5.4 NYU Abu Dhabi (NYUAD) — UAE

- **Website:** https://nyuad.nyu.edu/
- **What they do:** Branch campus of NYU with an Office of Research Translation and Entrepreneurship that advances academic research into practical applications.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** Global university brand, research excellence, and commercialization infrastructure in Abu Dhabi.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through the Office of Research Translation and Entrepreneurship.

### 5.5 Sultan Qaboos University — Oman

- **What they do:** Oman's premier national university. Hosts the UNESCO Chair in AI and Ethics. Active in AI-related research and education.
- **Why they are a strong test bed:** UNESCO Chair provides international credibility and research framework for AI in education pilots.
- **Partnership pathway:** Engage through the UNESCO Chair or university research office.

## 6. Cross-Regional Networks and Platforms

### 6.1 Global EdTech Testbed Network (GETN)

- **Website:** https://globaledtech.org/
- **What they do:** Collaborative initiative addressing challenges in the design, evaluation, and implementation of edtech testbeds. Brings together educators, researchers, policymakers, and technologists. Has hosted gatherings in Italy, UK, and Qatar.
- **Why they are relevant:** MENA stakeholders actively participate. Qatar has hosted GETN events. Provides standardized methodologies for evaluating edtech testbeds — participation legitimizes an organization's testbed capabilities.
- **Partnership pathway:** Participate in GETN events and apply principles to local testbed implementations.
- **Reference:** https://globaledtech.org/about/

### 6.2 EdTech Hub (Global, with MENA focus)

- **Website:** https://edtechhub.org/

2. **Saudi Arabia for scale:** Once a product is validated in Qatar, Saudi Arabia's 6M+ student AI curriculum rollout offers massive scale-up potential through MOE/SDAIA partnerships.

3. **Jordan for evidence generation:** QRF's evidence-based culture makes it ideal for generating the research data needed to unlock larger partnerships.

4. **UAE for funding and visibility:** ADQ/Gates Foundation partnership, Dubai Future Accelerators, and Abdulla Al Ghurair Foundation provide funding pathways and international visibility.

5. **Morocco and Egypt for emerging market validation:** Both countries are actively seeking foreign edtech providers and have large student populations.
